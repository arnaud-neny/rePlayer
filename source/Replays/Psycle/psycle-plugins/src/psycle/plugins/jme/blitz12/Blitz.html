Features

ComponentDescription
Oscillators
  • 4 Oscillators
  • 34 waveforms
  • 47 double buffered waveform modulation functions
  • oversampling
  • syncronizable
  • variable phase feedback
Ring Modulators
  • 2 Ring Modulators
  • can also do Amplitude Modulation if using RM-to-AM function
Arpeggiator
  • 12 Patterns
  • Shuffle
  • 2 types of optional retrig
  • special routing options for each oscillator
LFO
  • 8 LFO for symmetry control
  • 1 LFO for parallel routable vibrato
  • 1 LFO for filter sweeps
Envelopes
  • 2 ADSDR envelopes with keyscaling and velocity
  • 1 AD envelope for pitch modulation
Filter
  • 1 optional filter with 10 modes and keytrack

BFFF and lower Arpeggio
An Arpeggio is a classic method to simulate chords used in days where the soundchips could only do 3-4 voices. Instead of playing the notes simultanously, they are played one after one. To define an arpeggio, or short arp, you split the 16Bit parameter in three parts, say x, y and zz. x is the 2nd note, y the 3rd, and zz is optional for the 4th note. the notes are entered relative to the base note. so C-4 4700 gives C-4 E-4 G-4, the standard major chord. as said you can enter also a fourth note, so if you would enter C-4 4711 you get C-4 E-4 G-4 F-5. Using the routing in the oscillator options you can use this data also to route notes of the chords to transpose to individual oscillators.
